What is Eigenself

A 20-minute voice interview that produces a structured document describing who you are — how you think, what drives you, how you communicate. A file any AI can load and use on your behalf.

Who is it for

Anyone who feels the systems evaluating them — job applications, AI tools, hiring filters — don't actually understand who they are. Students, career changers, professionals navigating an AI-shaped world.

How does it work

You speak. Amazon Nova 2 Sonic listens and asks follow-up questions in real time. When you're done, a second model reads the full conversation and writes your identity protocol. You download it. You own it.

What do you get

A structured .md file — your identity protocol. Download it, read it, edit it. Load it into any AI agent as a system prompt and it can represent you accurately, not approximately.

Why does it exist

AI is already making decisions about people. It reads whatever it's given — keywords, job titles, a CV. It has no idea who the person actually is. Eigenself fixes that.

What makes it different

Not a profile. Not locked-in memory. A portable .md file you own — faithful to what you said, loadable into any AI, and built around how you think, not just what you've done.

Why this exists

AI is reshaping everything. The question is whether it understands you.

Most AI tools have some form of memory now — they remember things you've said, they recall past conversations. But that memory is passive. It accumulates in the background without you shaping it. You can't edit it, you can't audit it, you can't make it precisely represent who you are. And you certainly can't hand it to a different tool, or to someone else's AI agent.

There is a deeper problem too. AI is already making decisions about people — in hiring, in collaboration, in how opportunities are surfaced. And it reads whatever it's given. A list of keywords. A job title. A CV written for a world that no longer exists. It has no idea who the person actually is.

Eigenself gives you something different — a .md file that describes who you are: how you think, how you communicate, what drives you, what you value, where you draw the line. Written in your own voice, from a real conversation. A file you own, can read, can edit, and can load into any AI as a starting point. Not memory. Not a summary. A protocol.

eigen—

From German. Meaning own, peculiar to, characteristic of a thing and nothing else.

—self

A person's own being. Used reflexively — as in the self that belongs to no one but you.

Together: one's own characteristic self. Your intrinsic nature — not the version you perform for a recruiter, not the keywords on a CV. The thing underneath.

How it works

You speak. AI listens. A protocol is born.

Not a form. Not a questionnaire. A real conversation — unhurried, curious, adaptive. An AI agent conducts the interview. Another structures what it hears.

Eigenself conducts a 20-minute voice interview using Amazon Nova 2 Sonic, then synthesises everything you said into a structured .md identity protocol — a machine-readable document that captures not just what you have done, but how you think, what drives you, how you communicate, and what makes you specifically you.

The protocol is designed to be loaded into any AI agent as a system prompt. Once loaded, that agent can represent you accurately — not approximately. What you do with it from there is up to you.

There is something intentional about using AI — the very technology making the world move faster — to help you understand yourself more clearly.
